About Us

Burger King, established in 1954 in Miami, Florida, by founders James McLamore and David Edgerton, stands as one of the world's most iconic fast-food chains, celebrated for its flame-grilled burgers and customer-centric ethos. With a global footprint spanning over 19,000 restaurants across more than 100 countries, Burger King serves millions of customers daily, delivering bold flavors and affordable dining experiences. Its signature offering, the Whopper, introduced in 1957, remains a cornerstone of its menu, featuring a flame-grilled beef patty, fresh lettuce, tomatoes, onions, pickles, mayonnaise, and ketchup, all nestled in a sesame seed bun.

The brand’s “Have It Your Way” philosophy, launched in 1974, revolutionized fast food by prioritizing customer customization, allowing diners to tailor their meals to their preferences. Beyond burgers, Burger King’s menu has evolved to include crispy fries, chicken sandwiches, nuggets, plant-based options like the Impossible Whopper, and a robust breakfast lineup featuring items like the Croissan’wich. This adaptability reflects Burger King’s commitment to meeting diverse tastes and dietary needs while maintaining its reputation for quality and value.

Headquartered in Miami, Burger King operates under Restaurant Brands International, its parent company since 2014, alongside Tim Hortons and Popeyes. The chain has embraced innovation, from digital ordering and delivery services to eco-conscious initiatives like reducing packaging waste and sourcing sustainable ingredients. Burger King also engages communities through local partnerships and charitable efforts, reinforcing its role as a socially responsible brand. Known for its playful marketing, including memorable campaigns and cheeky social media presence, Burger King connects with younger audiences while staying true to its roots. Whether through limited-time offerings, bold new menu items, or global expansion, Burger King continues to ignite appetites and deliver unforgettable dining moments, solidifying its legacy as a fast-food pioneer.
